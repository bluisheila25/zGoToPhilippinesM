  • Also known as Mount Mayon and the mountain is a national park, a protected landscape in the country proclaimed as Mayon Volcano Natural Park in the year 2000. With its natural beauty, the "perfect cone" because of its almost symmetric conical shape however it is still one of the active volcano in the Philippines, Mayon forms the northern boundary of Legazpi City, Albay. According to the local folklore, it is known as Bulkang Magayon which means 'Beautiful Volcano' and it was named after a legendary heroin Daragang Magayon (Beautiful Lady). Mayon Volcano is the main landmark of Albay Province, Philippines, rising 2,462 metres (8,077 ft) from the shores of the Gulf of Albay about 10 kilometres (6.2 mi) away. Mayon is the most active volcano in the Philippines having erupted over 49 times in the past 400 years. The first record of a major eruption was witnessed in February 1616 and the most destructive eruption of Mayon occurred on February 1, 1814. Mayon Volcano's longest uninterrupted eruption occurred on June 23, 1897 which lasted for seven days of raining fire. The Mayon has been restless since 1999, when it unexpectedly emitted a large plume of ash startling the local residents. A series of eruptions followed in 2000 and 2001. Prior to this, the last major eruption of the Mayon was in 1993, causing the death of 70 people and evacuation of 50,000 residents. PHIVOLCS is still monitoring the stability of the Mayon Volcano on July 10, 2009, PHIVOLCS raised the status from Alert Level 1 then on October 28, 2009, a minor ash explosion lasting for about one minute occurred in the summit crater. November 11, 2009, a minor ash explosion occurred at the summit crater lasting for about three minutes. December 14, 2009, as a result of 83 volcanic quakes in the preceding 24 hours, Phivolcs raised the Alert status to Level 3 and on December 20, 2009, PHIVOLCS raised Mayon's status level to alert level 4 because of an increasing lava flow in the southern portion of the volcano and alert level 4 was maintained as the volcano remained restive through the month of December. On January 13, 2010, PHIVOLCS reduced the alert level from 3 to 2, saying that this indicated a further reduction in the likelihood of hazardous eruption.

It is a first class municipality and managed resource protected area in the province of Palawan. Situated in Bacuit Bay, it is composed of 45 islands and islets; each has its own unique geological formations and the highest peak is at Cadlao Island, towering up to 640 metres (2,100 ft) above sea level. In 1984, the Ministry of Natural Resources issued the Administrative Order No. 518, establishing a 360-square kilometer maritime area in El Nido as a turtle sanctuary. In 1991, the rest of the Bacuit Bay, including its island and islets, was proclaimed by the Philippine government as a marine reserve. El Nido, being one of the most diverse ecosystems in the country, it is protected for its unique flora and fauna, and pristine geologic formations. These include: limestone cliffs, 50 white sand beaches, 16 endemic and 10 threatened species of birds, 6 species of marine mammals endemic to Palawan (including dolphins and its native dugong), 4 species of endagered marine turtles (hawksbill, olive ridley, leatherback and green sea turtles), 100 species of corals and 813 species of fish. This made El Nido as one of the top visited destinations in Philippines.
